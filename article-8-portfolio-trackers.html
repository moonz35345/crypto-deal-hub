Why You Need a Portfolio Tracker

If you hold crypto on more than one exchange or wallet, you know the problem: logging into 4 different apps just to check your total balance. A portfolio tracker solves this by connecting to all your wallets and exchanges, giving you a single real-time view of your entire portfolio — including profits, losses, and asset allocation.

The best trackers also help with tax reporting, automatically categorizing transactions and generating tax documents. This alone can save hours of work at tax time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to connect my exchange to a portfolio tracker?▼
Yes, when using reputable trackers. Most use read-only API keys, which means the tracker can see your balances and transactions but cannot withdraw or trade. Never give a tracker withdrawal permissions. Always create a dedicated API key with read-only access.
What's the best free crypto portfolio tracker?▼
CoinMarketCap offers the most generous free tier — unlimited manual entries with no transaction limits. For automatic syncing, CoinStats offers 100 transactions free, and CoinTracker offers 25 transactions free. If you need tax reports on a budget, Koinly's free trial lets you test before committing.
Can a portfolio tracker help with taxes?▼
Yes. Trackers like CoinTracker and Koinly automatically categorize your transactions (buys, sells, trades, income), calculate capital gains/losses, and generate tax reports compatible with TurboTax, H&R Block, and other tax software. This can save hours of manual work. See our crypto tax guide for more.
