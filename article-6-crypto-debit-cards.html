What Is a Crypto Debit Card?

A crypto debit card works like a regular Visa or Mastercard — you can use it at millions of merchants worldwide. The difference? Instead of spending from a bank balance, the card automatically sells your crypto and converts it to local currency at the moment of purchase.

Most crypto cards also offer rewards — typically 1-5% cashback paid in crypto. This makes them an attractive option for people who want to use crypto for everyday spending without the hassle of manual conversions.

How to Choose the Right Crypto Card

Consider these factors when choosing a card:

  • Where you live: Not all cards are available in every country. The Krak Card is UK/EU only. Coinbase Card is US-focused. Crypto.com has the widest global coverage.
  • Which crypto you hold: If your portfolio is mainly Bitcoin, choose a card that supports BTC spending. If you hold altcoins, check the supported asset list.
  • Spending habits: If you spend heavily, look for higher cashback tiers. If you mostly want convenience, prioritize low fees.
  • Staking requirements: Some cards (Crypto.com) require you to lock up tokens for higher rewards. Make sure you're comfortable with that commitment.

Tax Implications of Spending Crypto

In most countries, paying with crypto is a taxable event. Because the card sells your crypto at the moment of purchase, that sale triggers capital gains tax if your crypto has appreciated since you bought it. For example, if you bought Bitcoin at $40,000 and spend it when it's worth $65,000, you owe tax on the $25,000 gain — even though you just bought groceries.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are crypto debit cards safe to use?▼
Yes. Crypto debit cards are issued by regulated financial institutions and run on Visa or Mastercard networks. They include standard fraud protection and can be frozen via the issuer's app if lost or stolen. Your crypto funds are held by the issuing exchange or in a secure custodial account.
Which crypto debit card has the best rewards?▼
Crypto.com offers the highest headline rewards (up to 5%), but requires significant CRO staking for top tiers. Coinbase Card offers up to 4% with no staking requirement. Krak Card offers up to 1% with the simplest fee structure. The "best" card depends on your location, spending habits, and whether you want to stake tokens.
Can I use a crypto card in my country?▼
Availability varies: Crypto.com is available in the widest range of countries (US, UK, EU, Singapore, and more). Coinbase Card is available in the US and parts of Europe. Krak Card is available in the UK and select EU countries. Bybit Card is available in the UK and EU. Always check the issuer's website for the latest country availability.
Is spending crypto with a debit card a taxable event?▼
In most jurisdictions, yes. When the card sells your crypto to pay the merchant, that sale is a disposal for tax purposes. If your crypto has increased in value, you'll owe capital gains tax on the appreciation. Learn more in our crypto tax guide.
