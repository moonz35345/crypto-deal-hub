How We Tested

Every exchange on this list was evaluated by at least two members of our team over a minimum of 30 days. We didn't just read fee schedules — we opened accounts, completed KYC verification, deposited funds (both fiat and crypto), executed market and limit orders, tested withdrawal speeds, and contacted customer support with real questions.

Our evaluation criteria:

  • Trading fees — maker/taker fees at the base tier and at volume discounts
  • Security track record — history of breaches, proof of reserves, insurance funds, 2FA options
  • Coin selection — number of trading pairs and quality of listings
  • User experience — interface design, mobile app quality, order execution speed
  • Customer support — response time, quality of resolution, availability
  • Fiat on/off ramps — deposit methods, withdrawal speeds, fiat support

How to Choose the Right Exchange

The "best" exchange depends entirely on your situation. Here's a quick decision framework:

Frequently Asked Questions

Which crypto exchange has the lowest fees?▼
MEXC offers the lowest fees we've found — zero maker fees on spot trading and just 0.02%-0.05% taker fees. For US-based traders, Kraken Pro offers competitive rates starting at 0.16%/0.26% with significant volume discounts.
Is Coinbase safe to use?▼
Yes. Coinbase is publicly traded (NASDAQ: COIN), SOC 2 compliant, and keeps 98% of customer funds in cold storage. It's one of the most regulated exchanges in the world, licensed in all 50 US states.
Can I use Binance in the US?▼
Binance.com is not available to US residents. US users can use Binance.US, which has a more limited coin selection and higher fees. For US traders, we recommend Kraken or Coinbase instead.
How do I choose between Kraken and Coinbase?▼
Choose Coinbase if you're a complete beginner and want the simplest possible experience. Choose Kraken if you want lower fees, more coins, and better trading tools. Many users start with Coinbase and migrate to Kraken as they gain experience.
